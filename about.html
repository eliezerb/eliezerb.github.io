About me

I'm a 24 years old Computer Science Ph.D. student at the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul based in Porto Alegre, Brazil. I was born in a small city named Erval Velho and I grew up on a farm helping my parents, until I decided to follow my passion for Computers doing a B.Eng. in Computer Engineering and an M.S. in Computer Science. In my free time I look for Open Source projects to contribute, read random classic books and papers from people I admire.

During my Undergraduate course at Universidade do Oeste de Santa Catarina, I worked as a research assistant with Professor Luiz Carlos LÃ¼ckmann for two years, identifying the profile of students at Community Colleges in Brazil's South Region using data mining techniques.

As a Senior Undergraduate student, I started working with Professor Karl Phillip Buhr developing algorithms to detect and track players in indoor sports. At this time in 2014, I decided to look for a Graduate course in Computer Vision, started to answer StackOverflow questions and to practice Aikido.

I moved to Porto Alegre in 2015 where I did my Master degree at the Institute of Informatics- UFRGS advised by Professor Sergio Bampi and Professor Jacob Scharcanski. The main topic of our research was the segmentation and classificion of melanomas using only macroscopic images. The results were published in the 2016 IEEE ICIP and presented in Phoenix, AZ with the support of the IEEE Signal Processing Society Travel Grant.

Since 2016, I'm pursuing my Doctorate degree advised by Professor Jacob Scharcanski in Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition at Institute of Informatics - UFRGS. In January 2017, I joined the Editorial Board of the XRDS: Crossroads, The ACM Magazine for Students as a Feature Editor for a 2-year commitment.
